# backlog-librarian — autonomous, zero-blast-radius backlog-hygiene digest

## Why — wildcard-proposed, owner-approved 2026-07-21

The backlog is scaling fast (68+ cards; +14 this session), and `wildcard` / `pathfinder`
will add more. We have optimised CREATION; nothing MAINTAINS the set, so it risks becoming
a graveyard. This is the **curate** half of autonomous PO hygiene — `wildcard` *creates*
(divergence → new card), `backlog-librarian` *maintains* the existing set.

## What

A scheduled **autonomous** job (away-mode-loop food — zero blast radius, like `wildcard`)
that reads the whole backlog and emits ONE **review digest**: detected overlaps /
duplicates, stale cards (untouched N weeks), missing cross-links, cards whose `depends-on`
is already satisfied, contradictory or orphaned readiness/status states. It **proposes**
hygiene actions; it **never edits, archives, or merges cards** without owner approval.

## Why it's safe to autonomize

Bounded, reversible output (a digest) — the same principle that makes `wildcard` safe, and
what makes it ideal `autotest-e2e-away-mode-drill` food.

## Distinct from (not a duplicate)

- `fleet-curation` — cross-project, owner-run; this is within-project + autonomous.
- `audit-advisory-interval` — audit staleness; this is backlog-card hygiene.
- `skill-drift-surfacing-and-refresh` — skills; this is cards.
- `wildcard` — CREATES new cards; this MAINTAINS existing ones. The pair = autonomous PO
  hygiene.

## Open questions

- Staleness threshold; digest delivery (Telegram? TUI?); schedule cadence + token budget.
- Overlap/duplicate detection method (embedding vs heuristic on facet + title + body).
- Whether it ever proposes concrete diffs or only flags.

## Non-goals

- No autonomous card edits / archives / merges — digest + propose only.
