# x6 — workflow alternatives refresh (shallow, contract-judged)

## Why

The 2026-07-16 `po-capabilities` receipt judged superpowers/Pocock-class skill
bundles high-ceremony and inward-facing, but the X6 umbrella explicitly forbids
inheriting that verdict — both Horus and the alternatives have changed
substantially since. The boundary inventory sharpened what a re-examination
must answer: these bundles are *inner-loop* systems (established SWE practice
adapted to agents — spec-first, TDD, review rituals) while Horus's workflow is
an *outer loop* (direction, resume, dispatch readiness); they conflict only in
the shared artifact zone where both want to own the plan/spec/backlog
document.

## Intended outcome

A fresh, shallow, cited re-examination of superpowers and a handful of other
recognized opinionated agent workflows *as they exist at scan time*, judged on
one rubric: **could this bundle own the inner loop and its spec artifacts while
the Horus contract fields (natively, or via a thin projection) stay
authoritative for resume and dispatch?** Enough outward evidence to choose
among the umbrella's four dispositions — keep exclusive / borrow selectively /
support profiles / drop the direction.

## Broad boundaries

Shallow sweep via the `market-scan` skill (shallow-by-default as of v0.0.73),
read-only, cited; depth escalates only on explicit owner request. Early
non-goals: no installation or execution of any third-party workflow (the swap
experiment is a separate later draft, pointed at a disposable repo or
pbi-ecosystem, never the production probe); no selector or profile schema
design; the 2026-07-16 receipt is cited as superseded background only.

## Open decisions for backlog-refine

- Which alternatives make the list, and what "recognized" requires as an
  inclusion bar.
- Whether the contract-compatibility rubric above is the whole rubric or one
  axis among few.
- What the receipt must contain for the disposition decision to be makeable
  without a second scan.
